Heritage & Historical Tour

The Ajanta Caves

The Ajanta Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage site, are a collection of 30 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India, renowned for their exquisite murals and sculptures dating from the 2nd century BCE to around 480 CE.

The Ellora Caves

The Ellora Caves, also known as Verul Leni, are a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring a complex of 34 rock-cut caves, showcasing Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain art and architecture, located near Aurangabad in Maharashtra, India

The Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an iconic arch monument in Mumbai, India, built to commemorate the landing of King George V and Queen Mary in 1911, and it stands as a symbol of both the British Raj and India's independence.

Raigad Fort

Raigad is a hill fort situated at about 25 Km from Mahad in the Raigad district. Chhatrapati Shivaji renovated this fort and made it his capital in 1674 AD. Raigad, seen in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, India, is a hill fort located in the city of Mahad. It is one of the strongest fortress on the Deccan Plateau and was historically referred to as Rairee or Rairy fort.

Devagiri Fort

Devagiri Fort, also known as Daulatabad Fort, is a historic hill fortress in Maharashtra, India, near Aurangabad, with a rich history dating back to the Yadava dynasty in the 12th century.
