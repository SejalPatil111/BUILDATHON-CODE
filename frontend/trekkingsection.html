Trekking & Adventure Tour

Kalsubai Peak

Its summit, situated at an elevation of 1,646 metres (5,400 ft), is the highest point in Maharashtra and hence it is known as the "Everest of Maharashtra".The highest peak in Maharashtra, Kalsubai's top is at a height of 1646 M offering panoramic views of the Sahyadris. Known as one of the most popular one day treks in the region, the Kalsubai trek offers so much to both nature and adventure enthusiasts.

Harishchandragad

Harishchandragad is a popular hill fort in the Ahmednagar district of Maharashtra, India, known for its challenging trekking routes and breathtaking views of the Western Ghats, with the Khireshwar route being a particularly scenic and moderate to difficult trek.

The Rajgad Fort

The Rajgad Fort is located around 60 km (37 mi) to the south-west of Pune and about 15 km (9.3 mi) west of Nasrapur in the Sahyadris range.The fort has stood witness to many significant historic events including the birth of Shivaji's son Rajaram I, the death of Shivaji's wife Saibai, the return of Shivaji from Agra, the burial of Afzal Khan's head in the Mahadarwaja walls of Balle Killa, the strict words of Sonopant Dabir to Shivaji.

Pratapgarh Fort

Pratapgarh Fort, near Mahabaleshwar, offers a moderate to difficult trek (2-3 hours) with historical significance and scenic views, best explored during the cool months (October-February) or monsoon for lush greenery.Pratapgad's watchtowers and bastions offer breathtaking panoramic views of the lush green valleys and dense forests that surround the fort. The trek leading to the fort is an exciting journey, with winding pathways cutting through thick greenery, making it a perfect adventure for trekkers and nature lovers.

Brahmagiri

The Brahmagiri trek near Nashik, Maharashtra, is a moderate trek starting from the Trimbakeshwar temple area, offering a 2-hour climb with around 500 steps to reach the summit, known for its scenic beauty and religious significance.
